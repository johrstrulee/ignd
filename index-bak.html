Contrary to popular belief there is no relationship between capitalism and democracy and neither has anything to do with the level of individual freedom or repression of a particular society. We associate communism and even socialism with repression and authoritarianism because of the soviet union but mostly because we have been told to. A country has an economic system ranging from laissez faire capitalism in which the means of production are all privately owned and the only motivation to make anything is profit to pure socialism in which the means of production are public or in the extreme communism which eliminates profit as a motivation and places all control of production in the governments hands. Socialism does not necessarily mean government control it just means public or group control so you could theoretically have an anarchist system of government with zero hierarchy and a socialist economic system in which the means of production are controlled by groups of volunteers that self-organize and make every decision democratically. For such a system to work a well designed framework with a minimal but carefully defined set of rules that guaranteed fundamental human rights, dignity, and equality, provided a mechanism to protect the vulnerable, and prevent any individual from gaining the power to circumvent the non-hierarchical nature of the society and the ability to control others and from hording extreme wealth. Leadership, being just a vital as it is in any society, would function by inspiring and motivating by example rather than fear, power and control.
